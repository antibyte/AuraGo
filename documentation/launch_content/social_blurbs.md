## 💼 LinkedIn (Professional & Architectural)

The intersection of generative AI and local infrastructure management has arrived. 

I’m excited to introduce **AuraGo**, an open-source (AGPLv3) AI agentic framework designed to bridge the gap between Large Language Models and self-hosted environments—with an absolute commitment to data privacy and security.

Most AI solutions are cloud-bound, presenting massive telemetry, configuration, and compliance leakage risks for private servers. AuraGo solves this by executing entirely on-premises.

**Architectural Highlights:**
1.  **High Efficiency**: Written in pure Go. Compiles into a single binary, running with an extremely small memory footprint suitable for edge nodes and Raspberry Pi hardware.
2.  **LLM Guardian Security**: Implements a dual-model system where a secondary security-specialized LLM monitors high-risk tool calls in real-time to defend against indirect prompt-injection.
3.  **Local AES-256 Vault**: Sensitive credentials and API tokens are encrypted locally and never exposed in raw text to the agent.
4.  **Multi-Tier Cognitive Memory**: Utilizes standard sliding-window SQLite conversation buffers, an embedded Go vector database (`chromem-go`) for RAG, and an operational Knowledge Graph of the network's assets.

By empowering AI with over 100+ native adapters (Docker, Proxmox, Home Assistant, TrueNAS, SSH, and Ansible), AuraGo acts as a secure, autonomous co-pilot for local sysadmins and home lab enthusiasts.
